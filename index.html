Accessibility

Explain tab order and its importance?

Tab order, is the order within online forms, that if you press tab it will cycle through. Typically elements are selected from top to bottom. You can change the order that items are selected using the attribute tabindex = "#" within an elements. The # represents the number in the order you want to cycle through elements starting with 0.

What is WCAG2 and WAI? Explain beyond the acronyms

WCAG2 is Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2. It is an recommendation for content to make them more accessible to a wider range of people with disabilities, with accommodations for blindness and low vision, deafness and hearing loss, limited movement, speech disabilities, photosensitivity, and a combination of these.

WAI is Web Accessibility Initiative. WAI was created by W3C and it seeks the make the web and software more accessible to disabled populations and the growing elderly population. It accomplishes this through "inclusive design" principals, which include; text alternatives for non-text items, present content multiple ways, ensuring content is easy to see and hear, design content so it appears in predictable ways.

Why use a color contrast analyzer?

An color contrast analyzer (CCA) helps you determine the legibility of text and the contrast of visual elements, such as graphical controls and visual indicators. It's purpose is to simulate color blindness to aid in development of websites and software. It helps developers ensure that fonts and images have a sufficient contrast so the visually impaired can understand the content.

What is User-centered design?

User-centered design (UCD) is a design process in which designers focus on the users and their needs during the design process.

What is usability?

Usability is the ease and access of a product or website with specified goals with effectiveness, efficiency and satisfaction.

What is the impact on users of increased brand awareness for users?

Brand awareness is the reputation of a company that effects how consumers see them. It's their ability to recognize your brand logo or name and the associations they have with you. Their relationships with brands also drive purchases.
